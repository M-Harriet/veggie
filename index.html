1. Marrow Vegetables

Helps us to reduce weight, improves our vision, makes our bones stronger, strengthens our immunity system and also helps to improve blood circulation and energy level.

Marrow is a summer squash that is a mature courgette left on the plant to grow longer. It is a member of the gourd section of cucumbers, along with courgettes, and is related to melons and squash. Marrows are long and cylindrical, with a firm rind and creamy flesh, and can be green, yellow, or a mixture of both. They have a mild flavor and edible skin and seeds, and can be used as edible casings for stuffings like mincemeat.

2. Organic Vegetables

What is meant by Organic Foods?

Organic food is grown without the use of synthetic chemicals, such as human-made pesticides and fertilizers, and does not contain genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Organic foods include fresh produce, meats, and dairy products as well as processed foods such as crackers, drinks, and frozen meals.



3. Leafy Vegetables

What are Leafy Vegetables?

Leaf vegetables, also called leafy greens, pot herbs, vegetable greens, or simply greens, are plant leaves eaten as a vegetable, sometimes accompanied by tender petioles and shoots. Leaf vegetables eaten raw in a salad can be called salad greens. Nearly one thousand species of plants with edible leaves are known.

4. Cruciferous Vegetables

What are Cruciferous Vegetables?

Cruciferous vegetables are vegetables of the family Brassicaceae with many genera, species, and cultivars being raised for food production such as cauliflower, cabbage, kale, garden cress, bok choy, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, mustard plant and similar green leaf vegetables.
